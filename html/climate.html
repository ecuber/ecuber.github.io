Los Angeles

Impacts LA Has Already Experienced

Climate change is not a new issue and has already had detrimental effects on the LA area.
The temperatures in the area have steadily been rising over recent years. Wildfires are becoming more and more common in the dry heat. There has also been a drought for many years throughout southern california as a result of the changing climate. This hurts the agriculture industry and the health of the ecosystem.

Future Impacts LA Will Experience

As a result of climate change, California residents are moving from their areas to places that are cooler on average, which hurts the economy. In addition, the sea level’s rising will potentially damage the iconic beaches and coastal areas in the Los Angeles area. The temperature is still on an increasing trend and is not slowing down. Finally, more wildfires are expected in the future.

What can be done to minimize climate change's impacts?

  • Reduce car emissions
  • Get drought tolerant landscaping
  • Force large companies to comply
  • Get rid of the HOT

What is already being done to minimize climate change's impacts?

  • Compensating drought tolerant landscape conversion
  • Limiting water use in homes
  • City bikes installed to encourage driving less
  • More public transportation routes being added
